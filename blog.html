Women Who Code NYC Meetup -- Lightning Talks

Tuesday, July 7 2015

Yesterday I had the chance to attend a Women Who Code meetup.

I saw 10 lovely ladies speak. It was my first event like this with all women, and I loved it. Diversity of all kinds of necessary for great collaboration, but there is something very relaxing about working with women, as a woman. One of the reasons I love programming so much is because of the lack of discrimination (compared to other professions atleast). Can you make it work? Great. I don't care who you are, what you look like, where or even if you went to college, just prove you have the skills. Being surrounded by action-oriented women extends this attitude of minimizing BS so that maximal creation and acceptance --even encourgement-- of failure dominates culture of thinking. (Maurya Couvares, the founder of ScriptEd , even spoke about the importance of learning how to fail in the context of her company)

Speakers discussed the technical-- js promises, ES6 features , overview of Swift, web accessibility -- and nontechnical alike.

One thing worth noting were 2 vibrant ladies who have recently started the company monarq-- a friendship app for women. More interestingly, however, they also started a women's hackathon which I definitely intend on going on in October.

Another women's oriented world-wide collaboritive event that was discussed was Womens Entrepretneurship Day .
